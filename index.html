Foundational Concepts

Understanding Human Behavior

The study of Human Behavior in a criminology context involves an exhaustive analysis of the conduct, actions, and reactions of individuals under various social and psychological stimuli. It is an interdisciplinary field that merges psychology, sociology, and biology. By examining why individuals deviate from social norms, criminologists can identify precursors to criminal activity.

The Science of Victimology

Victimology has evolved into a robust scientific field. It specifically investigates the relationship between the victim and the offender, the experience of being victimized, and the subsequent interaction with legal institutions. Unlike traditional criminology which focuses on the "offender," victimology prioritizes the "victim's" rights and recovery process.

In-Depth Course Learnings

Behavioral Models

  • Trait Theory: Predictors of criminal conduct through personality characteristics.
  • Social Disorganization: Crime as a product of broken social structures.
  • Differential Association: Learning techniques and motives through social interaction.
  • Biological Positivism: How physical factors alter behavioral responses.

Victimology Perspectives

  • Routine Activity Theory: Convergence of offender, target, and lack of guardian.
  • Victim Precipitation: Examining how behavior might trigger a criminal event.
  • Lifestyle Exposure: Daily habits dictating victimization risk.
  • Critical Victimology: State and law contributions to marginalized groups.

Trivia

What are the three elements of Routine Activity Theory?

A motivated offender, a suitable target, and the absence of a capable guardian.

What does Trait Theory suggest about criminals?

That specific physical, biological, or mental traits can predispose someone to crime.

Define Victim Precipitation.

A theory where the victim's own behavior or characteristics act as a catalyst for the crime.

What is the main focus of Victimology?

The scientific study of victims, including the relationship between victims and offenders.

Is Empathy a key cognitive factor in human behavior?

True. It is the ability to understand and share the feelings of another, crucial for social behavior.

Who is considered the "Father of Victimology"?

Benjamin Mendelsohn, who pioneered the scientific study of victims.
